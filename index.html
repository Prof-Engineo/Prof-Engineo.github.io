Three Algorithms Explained

I wrote three algorithms to "solve" this task assignement problem:

• An Evolutionary Algorithm

Makes clever abstractions from genetics and Darwin's theory of natural selection. A population of random solutions are created, the stongest solutions are refined, their attibutes are combined and passed onto future populations of soulutions.

• A Brute force Algorithm

Searches through every single permutation of the assignments and returns the highest "score". Although this will alway find the most perfect solutuion, it is very costly.

• A Random Selection Algorithm

This will randomly select a specified set of permutations and return the highest result.

Benchmarks and Performance

I encourage you to experiment, you'll notice that the EA often out-performs the Random Algorithm when we compare random genes created to random permutations selected.

You'll notice that the mutation rate becomes more important when working with lower generations and lower populations.

Conclusions

EA offers a middle ground between random and bruteforce algorithms.
EA will often find a better solution than random generation. Although it will not always find the best solution, it will find an "acceptable" solution much faster than if using a brute force method.

In the field of EA, diversity is IMPORTANT. Without diverstiy, without mutations, without diverse population crossover: We wouldn't solve the problem at hand.

How to Bypass Car Anti-Theft Systems (AKA: How to Hotwire a Car)

Why bypass the security on your own car?

  1. Ownership and Curiosity
    If you own your vehicle and enjoy understanding security mechanisms, this activity can be a fun challenge.

  2. Knowledge Is Power
    Understanding your car's security and ignition system is invaluable. This knowledge can help you perform DIY repairs and better protect your vehicle in the future.

  3. No keys
    If you've lost your keys, share your car with others, or have just salvaged a car without a key and need an inexpensive way to start it.

  4. Environmental Considerations
    Insurance companies often declare a lightly damaged car as a "total loss". This is because repair costs (like locksmith fees, paint, and dent repairs) often outweigh the value of salvaging the vehicle. These cars end in scrap yards sometimes without keys. Recycling and reusing these cars reduces waste, minimizes environmental harm from chemicals and cuts the carbon footprint associated with manufacturing new vehicles.

Why you SHOULD NOT bypass the security on your own car?

  1. Modern Security Systems Are Highly Effective
    Since 1998, EU legislation mandated immobilizer systems in UK cars, drastically reducing thefts. Approximately 97% of vehicle thefts involve using the owner's key or towing away the car. It is very clear that modern engine immobilizers work, and disabling it will increase your risk of car theft.

  2. Risk of Damage and Devaluation
    DIY modifications will lower the resale value of your car. Manipulating the ignition or steering lock might cause failures leading to a dangerous situation while driving.

  3. Insurance and MOT Complications
    A "hotwired" car may still pass its MOT if it has at least one alternative security device installed, but interpretations vary by tester. Also, such modifications could affect your insurance coverage, potentially leading to complications or invalid policies.

Overview of Vehicle Security Systems

Let's explore the key security features, generally speaking, so you can understand how they work:

  • External Security: Alarms and Door Locks

  • Internal Security: Ignition Key, Steering Lock, Immobilizer

External Security - Alarms and Door Locks

Alarms:
Most alarms trigger due to:

  • Motion or vibration sensors

  • Manual door unlocking without a valid wireless key

  • Ignition start attempts without a valid transponder

Disabling alarms:
To avoid disturbing your neighbours, you might want to disable the alarm. There at two potential ways in which you can disable a car alarm:

1. Kill the sound source. The Alarm system will be wired up to either the car horn, or its own speaker, or both. Find the horn and/or alarm speaker (you can find a diagram online for popular cars) and cut the wires.

2. Kill the power supply. Remove the alarm fuse or disconnect the battery. You might be able to find documentation online about the fuse location. The alarm may have its own backup battery.

Tip: You'll most likely need access to the interior of the car to disable the alarm. Ultimately the alarm will sound before you can disable it. Plan your timings wisely.

Example of an alarm system for a 2009 Kia Optima.

Door Locks:
There are several methods, some easier, some more destructive than others.

  1. Lock Picking: Possible but not recommended unless you're an expert with the right tools.

  2. Drilling: Use a tungsten carbide drill to destroy the lock cylinder, then manipulate the remaining mechanism. This is destructive and will require replacing the lock.

  3. Forced Similar Key: Insert a similar key and turn with a wrench to break the pins. Be aware this might leave the lock vulnerable to others with the same key shape. You can buy blank keys for your car online quite easily.

  4. Carefully Removing a Window: Use piano wire to cut the adhesive around a window, then carefully remove the glass.

    Example of a car window removal kit for sale online.

  5. Wedge Open the Door: Car doors are flexible; use this to your advantage. Pry the most flexible part of the door open just enough to insert some sort of tool to reach the unlock handle with.

  1. "Jimmy" Tool / "Jim Rod": Insert a hook through the window seal to manipulate the lock mechanism. This doesn't work on all cars as some have shields above the lock mechanism.

Internal Security - Ignition, Steering Lock and Immobilizer

Accessing the Internal Security:
Once inside the car, you will need to remove plastic covers around the ignition, steering lock, and immobilizer. Look for screws or force the casing off if needed.

Bypassing the Ignition Key:

Note: After bypassing the ignition, you'll still need to bypass the immobilizer circuit.

Forced Similar Key: Similar to external lock bypass, force a key into the ignition and turn with a wrench. If this method works for you then you will have also disarmed the steering lock.

Hot-Wiring:

1. Cut the ignition wires (Neutral, Accessory, On, Start).

2. Connect the Neutral, Accessory, and On wires together to power the system.

3. Then, briefly touch the Start wire to the Neutral to crank the engine.

You can find the color of the ignition wires online for your car. Usually Neutral is black.

You can figure out which wire is Neutral by checking for continuity between the wire and an earthed point in your car. Just make sure you disconnect the battery first.

Reconnect the car battery and you can then figure out the rest of the wires by connecting them to the Neutral wire one by one. The Start wire may produce a spark.

Removing the Steering Lock:

  • The lock will likely be attached with a security bolt like the image bellow. You can remove a security bolt by cutting a ridge into it with a cutting disc, then using a flat head screwdriver.

  • Some locks may have a locking pin; this should be drilled through with a tungsten carbide bit .

You'd need to drill through it to remove the lock from the steering wheel. The highlighted block on the right is the "latch" which locks the steering wheel in place.

Bypassing the Immobilizer System

Cars use transponder chips in keys that communicate with a sensor. The ECU (engine computer) verifies the chip before starting the engine.

The solution is quite simple: replace the ECU with a "de-coded" version that doesn't verify transponder chips. You can buy them online for most cars.

Some cars do not have a decoded ECU available. So the solution is to replace the ECU with one that comes with a set of valid transponder chips.

Some cars have an extra computer for the Immobilizer. In this case you'd need to source an ECU, the Immobilizer computer and the valid transponder chips all from the same car.

Replacing these components is quite straight forward; they don't require any soldering. They just simply plug into dedicated ports.

Other Security Considerations

Just some other security measures for you to consider.

  • VIN Numbers: Virtually every UK car has a VIN stamped on the chassis. These are logged in a national database. You need a valid VIN to pass an MOT. If your MOT tester suspects a tampered VIN number they are required to notify the local police. A criminal could attempt to clone another vehicle's VIN, but most MOT testers would spot this.

  • Number Plates and ANPR: The number plates on a stolen car are very likely to be caught by an ANPR camera. Criminals will clone the plates of a car (often from a picture online) to circumvent detection.

  • Chop Shops and Exporting: Stolen cars can be dismantled or exported. Serial numbers on high-value parts such as catalytic converters can aid in recovery efforts. Exported vehicles require valid VINs and plates, which require verification against a national database. However, criminals can illegally export cars to countries that do not check vehicle records thoroughly when importing.

Reasons why should install Debian via CLI?

  • Learn more about GNU/Linux.

  • Tailor made Debian installation.

  • Really, really freaking stable.

  • Lower hardware requirements.

Why not Arch?

Arch is a great distro, and I would recommend installing Arch at least once for the experience. However Debian has several distinct advantages.

  • It has a very stable base that uses older and well tested stable code.

  • Despite using older code, Debian gets the latest critical security updates.

  • A very wide and stable selection of packages from trusted sources.

  • Easy to use .deb installer packages are available from unofficial sources.

In short: Debian has a rock solid foundation, and is probably the most well supported distro.

Downsides of Debian:

These downsides can be remedied to an extent by installing Debian via CLI.

  • Debian doesn't have the latest features and performance gains. But does this really matter? If you have sufficient knowledge of Debian then you can choose to install the latest package(s) from an unofficial source. For example some PC gamers may choose to install the latest Linux-Kernel, Nvidia and Wine packages from non-debian sources in order to maximise gaming frame rates.

  • In my opinion the Debian installer sucks. Its not the worst installer, but it really undermines the rest of the distro. The customisation options are restricted to bloated environments, at which point you may as well use something more user friendly like Mint or Ubuntu.

  • You could just use Arch. Arch has a really good wiki page that explains the installation process very well. However, I personally find Arch to be too unstable. And I prefer ubiquity of the .deb packages.

Windows OS installer and de-bloat script.

This is compatible with both windows 10 and 11.

What is the script for?

This script is for automating nearly every step of the windows installation. To be specific this script will do the following…

  • Automatically set up partitions.

  • Install windows

  • Remove unwanted components via the official Microsoft supported DISM tool

  • Remove unwanted components and spyware via unofficial methods

  • Automatically set up users

  • Apply custom registry keys

  • Skip out of box experience

You are encouraged to tweak this script for your own needs. This can be used to create a very stable and de-bloated installation of windows. With most of the Microsoft spyware disabled.

How to use this script?

Once booted into your windows installation media, press SHIFT+F10 and then run this script from a .bat file

How does this script work?

  • DISKPART script to set up partitions

  • DISM to "apply" a windows image (.wim file) to your C drive

  • Runs a bunch of DISM component removal commands to removes and disable nearly every .appx and optional feature. FYI this is an official Microsoft tool.

  • Manually delates a bunch of directory and files associated with bloat and spyware.

  • Creates and "applies" a blank "answer" file. This is an official Microsoft tool used to skip several installation steps.

  • Adds a couple of special reg keys to bypass some installation steps.

  • Adds a new admin user via command line.

  • Adds a bunch of useful reg tweaks to the new installation.
